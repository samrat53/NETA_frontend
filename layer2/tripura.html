TRIPURA

Northeastern state known for natural beauty, hills, waterfalls. Influenced by Bengali and Tribal flavors in food. Located near Bangladesh. Humid subtropical climate. Rich cultural heritage with festivals like Kharchi Puja.

Ujjayanta Palace

Magnificent palace in Agartala, Tripura, commissioned by Maharaja Radhakishore Manikya in the early 20th century. Showcasing a fusion of Mughal and British architectural influences, it served as the royal residence. Today, it stands as a symbol of Tripura's rich history and houses the Tripura State Museum, offering insights into the region's cultural heritage.

Rudrasagar Lake and Palace

Spectacular water palace, cinstructed by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Debbarman Manikya in the 1930s. Situated in the middle of Lake Rudrasagar, it is an architectural marvel blending Hindu and Mughal styles. Neermahal served as a summer retreat for the royal family and continues to attract visitors with its serene surroundings and captivating beauty.

Unakoti

Ancient archaeological site located in Tripura, India. Known for its rock-cut sculptures and stone carvings dating back to the 7th-9th centuries. It is believed to have been created by a mythical sculptor called Kallu Kumhar. Unakoti is a popular pilgrimage site and showcases intricately carved idols of gods, goddesses, and celestial beings. With its unique and enigmatic sculptures, it is a treasure trove of historical and cultural significance.
