MANIPUR

Northeastern state with scenic landscapes and rich culture. Located near Myanmar. Pleasant climate. Famous lakes and hills. Unique Manipuri cuisine. Renowned for Manipuri dance and vibrant festivals like Lai Haraoba. Known as the "Land of Jewels" for its natural beauty and traditional handloom products.

Moirang

Moirang is a historic town in Manipur and is famous for being the place where the flag of the Indian National Army (INA) was first raised. The town is home to the INA Museum, which displays artifacts and memorabilia related to the freedom movement. Additionally, visitors can enjoy the scenic beauty of the Loktak Lake and visit the ancient Moirang Temple.

Loktak Lake

The largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, Loktak Lake is known for its floating islands called phumdis. The lake offers stunning views of lush green landscapes, traditional fishing communities, and the unique Keibul Lamjao National Park, which is home to the endangered Manipur brow-antlered deer.

Kangla Fort

Situated in Imphal, Kangla Fort holds great historical and cultural significance. It served as the ancient seat of power for the Manipuri kings and is now a popular tourist attraction. Visitors can explore the fort's various structures, museums, and serene gardens.
