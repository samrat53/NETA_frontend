ASSAM

Picturesque northeastern state with tea gardens, rich wildlife, and vibrant culture. Home to the Brahmaputra River and renowned for its Assam tea, and mesmerizing bihu festival and pitha. Diverse and enchanting, Assam offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Kaziranga National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the one-horned rhinoceros. Kaziranga National Park is known for its rich biodiversity and breathtaking landscapes, including vast grasslands, marshes, and dense forests. Visitors can enjoy jeep safaris and elephant rides to explore the park and spot various wildlife species.

Majuli

Majuli is the world's largest river island located in the Brahmaputra River. It offers serene and picturesque surroundings with lush greenery and traditional Mishing tribal villages. The island is known for its vibrant culture, ancient monasteries (Satras), and the opportunity to witness traditional dance and music performances.

Kamakhya Temple:

Located in Guwahati, Kamakhya Temple is one of the most revered and significant Hindu temples in India. Situated atop Nilachal Hill, it offers panoramic views of the city and the Brahmaputra River. The temple is dedicated to the goddess Kamakhya and attracts pilgrims and tourists alike for its architectural beauty and religious importance.
